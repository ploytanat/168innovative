## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Brand owners, product developers, and sourcing managers at Thai cosmetic companies. They arrive during business hours on desktop, often comparing multiple packaging suppliers, looking for a partner who understands both product specs and aesthetics.

**Secondary**: Startup skincare founders who need OEM/ODM support from concept to production — they want to feel like they're working with professionals who understand their industry.

**Job to be done**: Find packaging that fits their product → assess credibility and service quality → request a quote or sample as fast as possible.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Material. Precise. Design-forward.

168 Innovative sells *to* the beauty industry — the website must demonstrate that they understand what beautiful packaging looks like. It should feel like a premium packaging studio, not a generic B2B supplier.

**Emotional goal**: Confidence. Customers should feel "these people get it — they understand my product and my audience."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Light mode — business context, daytime browsing, needs to feel clean and professional.

**Visual tone**: Editorial meets precision catalog. Think of a well-designed materials library or a premium packaging supplier's lookbook — structured, authoritative, tasteful.

**Color system**:
- Background: Warm off-white (#faf8f5 → #f7f4f0) — clean, not clinical
- Primary accent: Warm copper/bronze (~#a07040 range) — evokes packaging materials, premium, distinctive
- Secondary: Dusty rose (#c47b8a) — existing brand color, keep as secondary
- Ink: Deep warm dark (#1d1f22) for maximum contrast on light backgrounds

**Typography**:
- English display: Bricolage Grotesque — distinctive, organic-geometric, feels like packaging studio branding
- Thai heading: Kanit — strong, bold numbers
- Body (both languages): Sarabun — readable, professional

**Reference**: Bold Thai business sites with social proof, product display, and clear CTAs — but elevated to premium cosmetics industry standard.

**Anti-references**: Generic corporate blue/gray, excessive glass morphism everywhere, nested cards inside cards, floating radial blob gradients competing for attention.

### Design Principles
1. **Material confidence** — Every surface should feel intentional and physical, like well-made packaging. No decorative flourishes that don't earn their place.
2. **Editorial hierarchy** — Use dramatic type scale differences. Not every heading is the same size. Break the grid for emphasis.
3. **Prove expertise through design** — The website sells packaging to beauty brands. It must look like it was designed by someone who understands those brands' standards.
4. **Convert, don't decorate** — Every section exists to move the customer toward a quote request or sample order. Social proof, clear CTAs, friction-free paths.
5. **Copper over blue** — Warm copper accent instead of generic corporate blue. It evokes anodized aluminum, brass, premium packaging materials.
